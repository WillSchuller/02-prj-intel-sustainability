Sustainability Through the Ages

Explore Intel's journey through time, discovering how our commitment to innovation has shaped a more sustainable future for technology and our planet.

1968

Intel Founded

Intel is founded by Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore.

The newly renamed company Intel Corporation begins its journey from NM Electronics, laying the foundation for innovation in semiconductors and consumer technology.

1971

First Microprocessor

Intel introduces the world’s first commercial microprocessor.

The 4004 launches the microprocessor revolution and enables the future of smaller, smarter computing devices.

1978

8086 Processor

The 8086 processor establishes the x86 architecture.

This landmark chip becomes the basis for countless PCs and servers, shaping modern computing for decades.

1985

386 Processor

Intel launches the 386 with 32-bit architecture.

The processor brings improved performance and multitasking capabilities to personal computers, marking a new era for users.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

Intel reaches its highest annual greenhouse gas emissions.

The company responds with investments in renewable energy, chemical abatement, and efficiency improvements to reduce impact.

2020

RISE Strategy

Intel launches its RISE sustainability strategy.

Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, Enabling (RISE) sets goals for climate action, water stewardship, and waste reduction through 2030.

2022

Net-Zero By 2040

Intel commits to net-zero emissions by 2040.

The target covers Scope 1 and 2 emissions across global operations, building on years of sustainability progress.

2023

Renewable Electricity

Intel reaches 99% renewable electricity worldwide.

This achievement helps reduce carbon emissions and supports the company’s long-term sustainability goals.

2024

Sustainability Summit

Intel hosts its first Sustainability Summit.

The event brings suppliers, officials, and industry leaders together to collaborate on sustainable semiconductor manufacturing.

RISE Strategy

Under its RISE strategy (Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, Enabling), Intel set ambitious 2030 goals — advancing industry-wide progress on climate action, water stewardship, and waste reduction.

Commitment

In 2022 Intel committed to achieving net‑zero greenhouse gas emissions from its operations (Scope 1 & 2) by 2040, supported by supplier agreements and industry partnerships.

Water & Waste

Intel conserves billions of liters of water annually and works with communities to restore watersheds. The company also focuses on reducing waste and improving recycling to strengthen circularity.
